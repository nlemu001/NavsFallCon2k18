Speaker

Laurence Koo

Laurence was born and raised in the Netherlands and studied dentistry at the Vrije Universiteit in Amsterdam. While in college, he met The Navigators and became involved as a student leader. After his graduation he worked part time on staff with The Navigators and part time as a dentist. After 6 years he left dentistry to focus on helping students and young professionals grow in their faith and become disciples of Jesus. Laurence has served in different leadership position in the student work of the The Navigators in the Netherlands for 14 years. Since september 2016 he started working for the missions department of the US Navigators. He is responsible for the training and development for new missionaries in their twenties before and on the field. He lives now in Colorado Springs. He is also an international speaker and consultant on the topic of sexuality.

Sessions

Morning Workshops

Spending Daily Time with God
Bryan Thigpen
How to get daily time with God even if you barely have time to pee! Some simple techniques for spending quality time with God in the busyness of life.
Spending extended time with God
Ryan & Ariel Mullarkey
Cultivating the discipline of dependence, humility, and rest.
Meditating on God’s Word
Dave & Kelly Sasaki
It is possible to spend a lot of time in the Bible without seeing a lot of change in your heart. Meditating on God’s Word can be a tool to help you let God change you from the inside out. In this workshop, we will talk about what meditating on God’s Word means, why it’s important, and ideas to help you make it a part of your life.
Ten Reasons To Be Devoted To Prayer
Don Allen
This workshop is designed motivate us to pray with clear examples and reasons from the Scriptures. We will also consider practical implications/applications.
Evangelism
Tobie Owl
Whether you’re scared of evangelism, love it, never have done it or fall somewhere in between, attend this workshop and you’ll learn what it means to be a person of influence and disrupt the status quo.
Lordship: Making Christ the Center of Everything
John Crossman
As believers, it's a temptation to make counterfeit gods out of our self-image, success in school, romantic relationships, etc. Yet, knowing and experiencing Jesus as Lord is of surpassing worth compared to any other basis of finding our identity. So how can we get to know Jesus as Lord? What does it mean to have Him as Lord?
The Goodness of God
Carlos Ortiz
Spiritual disciplines are important, and yet their effectiveness can be stifled by having an inaccurate perception of who God is. Come relate with others who've experienced seasons of spiritual dryness. Also, come learn more about barriers to believing God's goodness which make disciplines seem like they are all duty and no beauty.

Afternoon Workshops

Biblical Budgeting
Katie Redel
In a culture that encourages spending, what does the Bible have to say about how we should use our money? We will look at the Word and see what God says about handling our finances. We will also look at practical tips and resources to help make this a disciplined area life.
Following Jesus and Honoring your Parents
Tim & Kylie Chen
As we continue to grow more in our relationship with God and obey His calling for our lives, we may find that we are at odds with our parents' desire. Jesus calls us to "hate" our father and mother to follow Him, but He also says to "honor" them as well. In this workshop, we'll be talking about what God desires for our relationships with our parents, and what it looks like to live in both of these commands.
Navigating the Local Church
Tilon Pervenecki
"I’m in Navs right now, why do I need the local church?” The vast majority of our Christian fellowship will come through the local church. By learning about the importance of the church and becoming involved, we step into the vast treasures of life-long spiritual growth and the joy of creating a lasting impact in our local communities.
Savoring the Riches & Avoiding the Ditches of Christian History
Jonathan Fitzgerald
In our time together we will discuss the faith equipping need to know the legacies of the Christians who came before us. By learning from their strengths, struggles, and weaknesses we can be better prepared to carry the torch forward to the next generation of Jesus' disciples.
Call to Work
Kyu Ho Lee
How work is integral to our design and purpose.
God The Father's Plan For Reaching The Nations With The Good News !!!
Paul Reynoso
Is The Great Commission still Relevant? What is God’s plan for The Nations, and how could we participate? Come ask your questions of a veteran missionary, learn together as we dive into the scriptures and hear stories.
