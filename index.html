Speaker

Osaze Murray

Osaze Murray is a husband, a father, a child of God, and a person that is passionate about grace. The son of a pastor, he accepted Jesus Christ as his Lord and Savior at a young age however, it was not until he was in college that he understood grace and brokenness. Along with his lovely wife, Hadiza, he is the Navigators Campus Director at Bowie State University and they are the grateful parents of Lydia Amanda and Osaze Josiah. He is the former Director of the Navigators African American Network. Osaze has studied at Howard University, Palmer Theological Seminary and is a featured Gospel Recording artist that has released several albums and mix tapes. Through this ministry, Osaze formerly known as Oppose glorifies the Lord through the God given talent to rap biblical truth through music. Songs from his projects have been played on Philadelphia’s Radio One 103.9fm, Hampton Road’s radio stations, and countless Internet radio shows and videos heard and seen in other countries and continents. As an ordained minister he has performed weddings and was featured as an officiant on David Tutera’s My Fair Wedding TV show on WE tv. Osaze serves locally as an associate minister and member of the local church (Mt. Pleasant Baptist Church in Washington, DC Pastor Terry D. Streeter).

Living out your faith in a secular world
Chris Ballesteros
So you are trying to live out your faith on a college campus, but you are surrounded by parties, casual hook ups, and a whole bunch of other influences. Philippians 1:27 says “let your manner of life be worthy of the gospel of Christ”. So how the heck do we do that when we live in the environment we do? In this workshop we will be looking at the realities of the world we live in, and how we can not only navigate through the world, but how we can live in a way that honors both ourselves and God.
Praying Scripture Powerfully
Bryan & Erin Thigpen
How important is prayer and how should we pray? The Lord Jesus spent a lot of time in prayer and so should we. If we want to be people who will effectively change the world than we need to learn how to pray more boldly. Come learn to be more bold and powerful in your prayer life by examining what The Bible shows us about it. Join us as we learn some unique tools and techniques in order to pray more powerfully like our Savior.
Discipleship Essentials: One Beggar Showing Another Where to Find Breadcrumbs
Alicia Garcia
Discipleship can seem scaring when at the core we are sharing what we have learned and experienced from God with others. Come spend time together growing a heart for the vision and a few practicals to make disciples as you make Him known.

Afternoon Workshops

Did Jesus really rise from the dead?
TJ Hayes
Have you ever felt intimidated when people challenge your faith in Christ? Did you know that the apostle Paul said that if Christ has not been raised, then our faith is in vain? Come strengthen your faith by learning a few of the many evidences for Jesus' resurrection.
What is God's will for my life?
Ryan Mullarkey
What does God want you to do with your life? Should you take that job you interned for? Does God want you to marry that person you've been dating? Psalm 25:14 says "The Lord confides in those who fear him..." In this workshop we will explore the difference between God's specific and general will, how to filter our decisions through God's Word, and how following God by faith is the most satisfying place we can be.
Getting started with Extended Time Alone With God.
Carlos Ortiz
Eternal life is knowing God. Spending extended time alone with Him is a great way to do that. Come develop the transformative habit that enables you to reprioritize your earthly life toward heavenly pursuits. Why? Because HE is so worth it!
Finding Hope in the Midst of Anxiety
Dave & Kelly Sasaki
Do you or someone you care about struggle with anxiety? Jesus cares, and offers healing, not condemnation. In this workshop we will discuss not only practical ways to deal with anxiety, but also ways to bring Jesus and his Word into your struggle.
